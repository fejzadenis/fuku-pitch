§ 01 / Audit

What's broken
right now

An external scan of bets.fuku.ag surfaces a small set of high-leverage fixes that cost almost nothing but compound every week left unfixed. Each finding below is observable from the public-facing site as of May 18, 2026.

— 一
IDENTITY

The site still says CBB Predictions.

Page title, meta description, OG card — all anchored to college basketball. The World Cup pivot is invisible to every Twitter scroll, every Google snippet, every link preview.

<title>CBB Predictions</title>
<meta description="Let's hammer.">
— 二
SEO

The HTML ships empty.

The site is a fully client-rendered SPA. Crawlers see metadata and nothing else. Google can't index tipsters. Twitter can't preview matches. Telegram can't unfurl picks. Every share is unstyled garbage.

— 三
VIRALITY

One static OG image for the entire site.

Every share of every page produces the same preview card. The single biggest free distribution channel in crypto — a screenshot-worthy slip — is unbuilt.

— 四
BRAND

The Fuku ecosystem isn't connected.

Nothing on bets.fuku.ag signals it's part of fuku.ag (FukuAI), brain.fuku.ag (Trading Terminal), or the $FUKU token. A pre-built Solana audience sits one click away and isn't routed in.

— 五
PRODUCT

The tipsters are invisible.

The private API is the moat. Externally, there are no tipster pages, no leaderboard URLs, no verified-ROI cards. The single most defensible asset of the product is not surfaced anywhere a stranger can find it.

— 六
POSITION

"Let's hammer." is a tagline with nowhere to live.

The phrase is sharp, on-brand, and degen-friendly. It's also the only piece of identity that survives the metadata. Build the entire surface around it — Hammers of the day. Hammer score. Hammer history.

§ 02 / Strategy

Three pivots
that change everything

The original brief assumed a generic crypto sportsbook chasing European football fans. The actual surface tells a different story. Get the positioning right and the rest of the roadmap halves in difficulty.

人
Audience · 01

Solana degens, not European football fans.

The Fuku ecosystem audience is already crypto-native, already trained on signals and alerts, already on X. Tap them first.

It means X bot before Telegram bot. Phantom-style share cards. SOL and USDC-on-Solana first. Wallet-based referral. Pump.fun aesthetic on the slip art.

運
Product · 02

Tipster platform with bets attached — not a sportsbook with content.

Users do not return for odds. They return for people, picks, and the leaderboard that ranks them honestly.

Every dev hour spent improving the book itself is an hour stolen from the only thing that makes Fuku different from fifty other .ag domains. Treat the sportsbook as plumbing.

福
Token · 03

Give $FUKU utility — don't launch anything new.

A token already exists. Currently it has no product use. Wire it into the product as the only crypto-native loyalty surface that costs zero legal time.

Hold X $FUKU → premium tier. Win prediction games → $FUKU drips from the dev wallet. Referral cuts payable in $FUKU. No new mint. No new audit. No new lawyer.

§ 03 / Moat

The tipster system
is the product

Everything else exists to amplify a verified pick into a shareable, tail-able, brag-able moment. If only five features ship in sixty days, they are these five.

"The only crypto book where you can see exactly who's been right and tail them in two taps."

That's the positioning. Everything in this section earns its place by making that sentence more true. The unit of trust is the tipster, not the platform. The unit of virality is the slip, not the bet. The unit of retention is the streak, not the deposit.

  • 一

    Tipster profile pages.

    Public, indexable, branded. Verified ROI, hot/cold last-10 indicator, follow button, tail history.

  • 二

    One-tap tail-bet flow.

    From pick card to placed bet in under five seconds. Stake prefilled to user's average. Confirm. Done.

  • 三

    Leaderboard with composite scoring.

    Adjusted ROI · consistency · recency · specialty. Components visible. Argument-bait by design.

  • 四

    X bot + Telegram bot.

    Daily pick DM. Bet confirmation. Pick-of-the-day public channel. VIP-gated channel. Account-linked.

  • 五

    Dynamic slip-share cards.

    Auto-generated PNG on every settled bet. Branded watermark. Scan-to-tail QR. Win-images share themselves.

§ 06 / Stack

Lean stack,
managed edges

Every infrastructure decision below is filtered through one question: does this break under match-day load when there's one person on call? The answer is "no" only when boring, managed, and cached.

Application

Existing core + thin layer.

  • Sportsbook core — untouched
  • Backend — Node/TS module added
  • Frontend — Next.js App Router (SSR/ISR for SEO)
  • Auth — Phantom + email link
  • LLM — Anthropic API, heavily cached
Data

Postgres + Redis. No more.

  • Postgres — tipsters, picks, follows, missions
  • Redis — sorted-set leaderboards, sessions, hot cache
  • BullMQ — notification queue, settlement jobs
  • Cold storage — weekly archive of old picks
Realtime

Don't build it. Buy it.

  • Ably or Pusher — live chat + odds delta channels
  • Web Push API — browser notifications
  • Telegram + X — own bots, low effort
  • Cloudflare — edge cache for public pages
Distribution

Where the audience already lives.

  • X — primary virality channel (Solana-degen audience)
  • Telegram — secondary, deeper engagement
  • Discord — webhook-only, no full server
  • Email — digests only, never transactional
Images

Slip cards as a first-class system.

  • Satori + Resvg — server-rendered PNG, fast
  • Caching — by bet outcome, served forever
  • OG endpoint — dynamic per tipster, per pick, per match
Observability

Boring tools, picked early.

  • Better Stack or Axiom — logs + uptime
  • Sentry — frontend + backend errors
  • PostHog — product analytics, self-host if needed
  • One on-call rotation — you
§ 07 / Survival

Cup ends.
The product continues.

Most World Cup pivots die on July 20. The 10–20% of cup tourists who followed a tipster, joined a Telegram, and built a streak become 80% of post-cup revenue. Build the cup for retention into August — not for handle in July.

The 39 days of the cup are not your revenue window. They are your customer acquisition window for the next twelve months.

The whale who comes for Brazil–Argentina but stays for Premier League is worth thirty World Cup tourists. The Telegram subscriber retained from the group stage is worth more than the casual who deposited $200 and left.

Every operational decision during the cup should answer one question: does this give us users in August? Loud wins, tipster relationships, and streaks are how you say yes.

§ 08 / Insights

The three sentences
that matter

If everything else in this document is forgotten, these three insights are the ones the whole plan turns on.

01
Product Insight

Users return for people, not platforms.

The sportsbook is plumbing. The tipsters and the community arguing over their picks is the product. Every hour spent on the book itself is stolen from the only thing that differentiates Fuku from fifty other crypto books on the same domain extension.

02
Business Insight

The cup is acquisition, not revenue.

Most operators optimize the cup for gross handle in July. Wrong window. Optimize the cup for retention into August. The user who stays through the EPL season opener is worth thirty cup tourists who deposit once and disappear.

03
Technical Insight

Ship velocity must hit zero on June 10.

The biggest technical risk isn't a feature failing — it's a match-day deploy taking down live betting during a Brazil game and torching trust permanently. Build monitoring and incident playbooks in Week 4. Practice an incident. Then freeze.
